Decimal Value

Before we move to decimal value we should first touch on just regular old value. Value is a concept that people are familiar with from a very young age, so some of the following content may seem redundant. The definition of value can get very philosophical, but essentially something has value in the context of mathematics if it can be represented as a number. This definition may raise controversy, but for the sake of keeping things easy to comprehend at this stage, we'll stick with it. Now lets look at the value of some digits.
The value of one in this case means a single grey circle, the value of two means a pair of grey circles and so on and so forth. This means that the digit one represents singularity, whether that is a physical object, an idea, or something further abstract. Similarly, the digit two represents duality, the digit three representing a triple, and so on. To keep things from getting too confusing here is the best way to put it, in mathematics we associate a digit, a symbol, with a certain value, case closed.
Alright, but what does decimal value mean? Well, we now have a better understanding of value, but to really understand decimal value we need to know what the word "decimal" means. The word "decimal" is commonly used to descibe our number system. If you do a quick Google search for the word decimal, it will spit out something along the lines of relating to ten or the power of ten. This is how our number system works, after you work through all ten of the digits, in order to keep counting up, you have to add another place. Since we have ten digits we go through all ten numbers before moving on to the next place, also called a decimal place. Hopefully this next animation will help make better sense of things.
